How VisionAI Works

A simple guide to how your browser learns to see.

1 The Concept: Transfer Learning

Training an Artificial Intelligence from absolute scratch takes thousands of hours and millions of images. Our app uses a clever shortcut called Transfer Learning.

Instead of starting with a blank brain, we start with MobileNet—a highly advanced Google AI model that has already been trained to recognize millions of everyday objects like cars, trees, shapes, and textures.

2 The Learning Phase (Feature Extraction)

When you upload pictures of your custom classes (e.g., your "Cats" and "Dogs"), we don't teach the model what a cat is from scratch.

Instead, we run your image through MobileNet, but we stop it right before it guesses what it is. At that point, MobileNet spits out a complex mathematical summary of the image's "features" (shapes, colors, edges). This summary is called an Activation Vector.

[Your Image] → [MobileNet Cortex] → [Math Summary of Image]

We then take that math summary, and map it to the label you gave it ("Cat"). We store all these summaries in memory.

3 The Prediction Phase (K-Nearest Neighbors)

When you upload a brand new test image to predict, we do the exact same thing: run it through MobileNet to get its math summary.

Then, we use a simple but powerful algorithm called K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN). The algorithm looks at this new summary and compares it to all the training summaries you uploaded earlier.

It essentially asks: "Which training images is this new image mathematically closest to?"

New Image Math Summary
↓
Closest to: [Cat, Cat, Dog]
↓
Prediction: Cat (66% Confidence)

If its features match closest to the "Cats" you uploaded earlier, the app confidently predicts "Cat"!
